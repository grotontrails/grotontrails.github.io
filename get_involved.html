Come Meet Us

Each month the Groton Trails Committee meets to discuss the status of ongoing projects, plan new projects, schedule work parties, update our trails projects list of current and future tasks, and report on problems and discuss solutions. The current meeting schedule calls for the meeting on the 3rd Tuesday of every month at 7:00pm at Groton's Legion Hall, 75 Hollis Street. It is always advisable to check the Groton Town web site for meeting updates or use the email address below for confirmation. The meeting is open to the public and visitors are always encouraged to attend and participate. If you have a specific topic to discuss please email the Trails Committee trails@townofgroton.org to get a slot assigned on the agenda.

Join our low volume email list to learn about and discuss the trails of Groton including activities, work parties, and trails advocacy. We will use this list to notify you of our guided hikes, bike rides, ski or snowshoe events, and similar excursions. When we organize work parties we will also post a call for volunteers using this list.

As a group member you can post questions and answers on your favorite trail, trail conditions (e.g. "there is a tree down on the XYZ trail"), if you have suggestions for a trail reroute around a wet spot, or similar.

To join please send an email with your full name to trails@townofgroton.org, and we will send you an invitation. This way we can keep spammers away.

The archive of the email list is available on the Friends of Groton Trails Network Google Group.

Eagle Scout Projects

Hello future Eagle Scouts! If you want to do a project for the Trail Committee please read through this, then if you are ready, contact us through email to set up a meeting. When you come to a meeting please show up with rough plans and a marked up map.

Here are some things to consider before coming to a meeting. What are your materials? How are you getting the materials? Where are you putting your project? Who is the land owner? Do you have their approval? How are you going to transport your materials/project in? Do you need an RDA (WPA/request for determination of applicability form)? Please follow the RDA Form instructions carefully. Note that RDA's take at least 3 months to be processed. Please consider if you are building a bridge, what is your foundation? Is it usable by horse or bike? How close is it to wetlands? Thank you for taking the time to read this through.
